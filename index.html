🔔 This is a preliminary workshop website prepared for the NeurIPS 2026 workshop proposal submission. Additional details, submission instructions, and schedule updates will be announced following workshop decisions.

About

LoRe4Health focuses on longitudinal reasoning systems capable of integrating evolving multimodal clinical evidence across patient trajectories. The workshop brings together researchers working on multimodal reasoning, retrieval-grounded inference, uncertainty-aware clinical AI, deployment challenges, clinician supervision, and trustworthy healthcare AI systems.

The workshop emphasizes challenges involving temporal reasoning, calibration under distribution shift, evidence attribution, longitudinal decision support, and deployment of multimodal foundation models in real-world healthcare environments.

LoRe4Health 2026 will be the inaugural edition of the workshop.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions on longitudinal clinical reasoning, multimodal healthcare AI, retrieval-grounded inference, temporal reasoning systems, uncertainty-aware clinical decision support, healthcare foundation models, deployment studies, clinician-AI interaction, and trustworthy AI for healthcare.

Submission instructions and important dates will be announced soon.

The workshop plans to use OpenReview for submission management and peer review.

Topics of Interest

  • Longitudinal clinical reasoning
  • Multimodal healthcare foundation models
  • Retrieval-grounded clinical inference
  • Temporal reasoning over patient trajectories
  • Calibration and uncertainty estimation
  • Clinical deployment and monitoring
  • Clinician-AI interaction
  • Federated and privacy-preserving learning
  • Trustworthy and interpretable healthcare AI
  • Benchmarking and evaluation of clinical reasoning systems

Submission Format

The workshop will accept non-archival papers and extended abstracts related to longitudinal clinical reasoning, multimodal healthcare AI, temporal inference, retrieval-grounded decision support, trustworthy clinical AI, and deployment studies.

Accepted submissions will be presented as posters, with a selected subset invited for spotlight presentations.

Submission details and OpenReview instructions will be announced following workshop decisions.

Sponsorship, Awards & Travel Support

The organizers are actively pursuing industry and institutional sponsorship to support student participation, diversity initiatives, travel awards, outstanding workshop contributions, and community-building activities.

We also aim to provide travel support and recognition awards for students and early-career researchers, particularly participants from underrepresented groups and regions.

The workshop is committed to fostering diversity across institutions, geographic regions, career stages, and underrepresented communities.

Can Longitudinal Clinical Reasoning Systems Be Trusted in Healthcare?
